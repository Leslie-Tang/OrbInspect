\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper presented OrbInspect, a ROS~2 and Gazebo Harmonic framework for
coverage-constrained autonomous orbital inspection. The framework keeps
CW/HCW dynamics as the state source of truth, uses Gazebo for visualization,
and records the artifacts needed to audit planning, control, coverage, safety,
and experiment configuration. The updated mesh-aware offline experiment
generated a feasible 1840~s set-cover CW tour with 23 selected viewpoints,
8.7697~m/s planned cumulative $\Delta v$, 7.5323~m minimum clearance, and
98.33\% final coverage over 180 mesh targets. Compared with random-safe and
several greedy baselines, the proposed method provides the best feasible
coverage-energy trade-off in the reported benchmark. The ROS smoke run
validated the logging and safety-monitoring pipeline, and Gazebo frames and
videos validated the visual replay interface.

The next technical steps are broader Monte Carlo studies, higher-fidelity mesh
occlusion acceleration, uncertainty-aware keep-out margins, camera slew and
illumination constraints, and optional Basilisk validation after the HCW
baseline remains reproducible. With those additions, OrbInspect can mature
from a reproducible inspection-planning framework into a high-confidence
experimental platform for autonomous inspection of large orbital structures.
