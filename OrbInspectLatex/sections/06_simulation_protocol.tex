\section{Experimental Protocol}
\label{sec:simulation}

\subsection{Offline Planning Benchmark}

The primary quantitative planning artifact is the local high-coverage offline
experiment directory. It uses a mesh geometry backend, CW/HCW dynamics,
180 area-weighted mesh targets, 357 candidate viewpoints, a 70\% coverage
success threshold, and a 98\% planning stop ratio. The experiment compares
\texttt{set\_cover\_cw\_tour}, \texttt{proposed\_safe\_cw\_nbv},
\texttt{coverage\_greedy}, \texttt{safe\_coverage\_greedy},
\texttt{distance\_greedy}, \texttt{fuel\_greedy}, and
\texttt{random\_safe}. The planner writes raw CSV files, configuration
snapshots, figures, and summary documents under the paper directory.

\subsection{ROS~2 Execution Smoke Run}

The ROS execution artifact is the local phase-10 smoke directory. The manifest
records the \texttt{full\_station} scenario, \texttt{greedy\_nbv\_safety\_filter}
method, and \texttt{hcw} dynamics backend. The run records odometry, nominal
control, safe control, coverage map, mission event, safety status, and planner
event topics. Its purpose is to validate that the ROS system produces the
paper-grade artifact layout and that the safety and logging interfaces behave
correctly. The updated validation launch can additionally replay the saved
high-coverage trajectory and attitude stream for RViz and Gazebo visualization.

\subsection{Gazebo Harmonic Validation}

Gazebo Harmonic is used to visualize the station and chaser. The validation
artifacts are stored in the local video-capture directory and include preview frames
and videos from chaser-follow and full-ISS views. Gazebo does not determine
the spacecraft state in these experiments. Instead, chaser pose is driven from
the ROS state stream, preserving the principle that the dynamics node is the
source of truth.

\subsection{Monte Carlo Baseline Table}

The primary method comparison is the deterministic mesh experiment described
above. The older Monte Carlo directory is retained as a small regression
artifact, but the paper's quantitative claims are based on the high-coverage
mesh comparison.
