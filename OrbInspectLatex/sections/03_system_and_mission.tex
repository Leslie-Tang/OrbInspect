\section{System Model and Mission Setting}
\label{sec:system}

\subsection{Reference Frames}

The inspection scenario is expressed in a local-vertical/local-horizontal
(LVLH) frame attached to a nominal target orbit. The target structure is
treated as fixed in this frame during one inspection pass. The chaser state is
\begin{equation}
  x =
  \begin{bmatrix}
    r_x & r_y & r_z & v_x & v_y & v_z
  \end{bmatrix}^{\mathsf{T}},
\end{equation}
where $r \in \R^3$ and $v \in \R^3$ are relative position and velocity in LVLH.

\subsection{Relative Dynamics}

For the baseline planner, chaser motion follows the
Clohessy--Wiltshire equations with control acceleration
$u=[a_x,a_y,a_z]^{\mathsf{T}}$:
\begin{align}
  \dot r_x &= v_x, &
  \dot r_y &= v_y, &
  \dot r_z &= v_z, \\
  \dot v_x &= 3n^2 r_x + 2n v_y + a_x, &
  \dot v_y &= -2n v_x + a_y, &
  \dot v_z &= -n^2 r_z + a_z,
\end{align}
where $n$ is the chief mean motion. The discrete propagation map is denoted
\begin{equation}
  x_{k+1}=f_{\mathrm{CW}}(x_k,u_k,\dt).
\end{equation}
This model is appropriate as a first-order relative-motion baseline for
near-circular-orbit inspection studies and is consistent with standard mission
engineering practice \citep{wertz2011space}.

\subsection{Inspection Target Model}

Let $\mathcal{T}=\{1,\ldots,N\}$ be a finite set of surface inspection targets
sampled from the station geometry. Each target $i$ has a surface point $p_i$,
normal $n_i$, and dwell-time accumulator $\tau_i$. A target is inspected when
it is visible for at least a required dwell time $\tau_{\min}$.

The current implementation can generate targets from simplified station
primitives. The planned paper algorithm should extend this to mesh-aware target
sampling on the real ISS model, with primitive geometry retained as a
conservative safety envelope.

\subsection{Mission Objective}

The mission starts from a safe initial relative state $x_0$ and terminates when
the required coverage threshold is achieved or when a maximum mission time is
reached:
\begin{equation}
  C_K = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N} y_{i,K} \ge C_{\mathrm{req}},
\end{equation}
where $y_{i,K}\in\{0,1\}$ indicates whether target $i$ has been inspected by
the final time step $K$.
