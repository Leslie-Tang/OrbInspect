\section{Problem Formulation}
\label{sec:problem}

\subsection{Visibility Conditions}

A target $i$ is visible from chaser pose $x_k$ if all of the following hold:
\begin{align}
  \rho_{\min} \le \norm{p_i-r_k} \le \rho_{\max}, \label{eq:range}\\
  p_i \in \fov(r_k,q_k), \label{eq:fov}\\
  \arccos\left(
    \frac{(r_k-p_i)^{\mathsf{T}}n_i}
    {\norm{r_k-p_i}\norm{n_i}}
  \right) \le \theta_{\max}, \label{eq:view-angle}\\
  \los(r_k,p_i,\mathcal{O}) = 1, \label{eq:los}
\end{align}
where $\rho_{\min}$ and $\rho_{\max}$ define the camera range, $q_k$ is the
camera attitude or assumed station-facing boresight, $\theta_{\max}$ is the
maximum allowable viewing angle, and $\mathcal{O}$ is the set of occluding
station primitives or mesh triangles.

The dwell-time update is
\begin{equation}
  \tau_{i,k+1} =
  \begin{cases}
    \tau_{i,k}+\dt, & \text{if target } i \text{ is visible at } k,\\
    \tau_{i,k}, & \text{otherwise},
  \end{cases}
\end{equation}
and the inspection indicator is
\begin{equation}
  y_{i,k}= \mathbf{1}\{\tau_{i,k}\ge \tau_{\min}\}.
\end{equation}

\subsection{Safety and Actuation Constraints}

The planner must satisfy acceleration, speed, and keep-out constraints:
\begin{align}
  \norm{u_k}_2 &\le u_{\max}, \\
  \norm{v_k}_2 &\le v_{\max}, \\
  d_{\mathcal{S}}(r_k) &\ge d_{\mathrm{safe}},
\end{align}
where $d_{\mathcal{S}}(r_k)$ is the signed distance from the chaser to the
station safety envelope.

Additional mission constraints may include terminal conditions, sunlight
constraints, communication geometry, plume impingement constraints, and camera
slew-rate limits. These are outside the first implementation but should be
represented in the final paper discussion.

\subsection{Coverage-Constrained Trajectory Optimization}

The full inspection planning problem can be written as
\begin{align}
  \min_{\{x_k,u_k,q_k\}_{k=0}^{K}}
  \quad &
  w_t K\dt
  + w_u \sum_{k=0}^{K-1} \norm{u_k}_2\dt
  + w_s \sum_{k=0}^{K} \phi(d_{\mathcal{S}}(r_k))
  + w_c (1-C_K) \label{eq:objective}\\
  \text{s.t.}\quad &
  x_{k+1}=f_{\mathrm{CW}}(x_k,u_k,\dt), \\
  & \norm{u_k}_2 \le u_{\max}, \\
  & \norm{v_k}_2 \le v_{\max}, \\
  & d_{\mathcal{S}}(r_k) \ge d_{\mathrm{safe}}, \\
  & y_{i,k} \text{ follows the visibility and dwell model},\\
  & C_K \ge C_{\mathrm{req}},
\end{align}
where $\phi(\cdot)$ is a soft safety penalty used only when a relaxed version
of the problem is solved. A hard-constrained implementation should enforce
$d_{\mathcal{S}}(r_k)\ge d_{\mathrm{safe}}$ at all times.

This problem is mixed-integer and nonconvex because of visibility, occlusion,
and coverage indicators. Therefore, the proposed first paper algorithm uses a
structured offline decomposition.
