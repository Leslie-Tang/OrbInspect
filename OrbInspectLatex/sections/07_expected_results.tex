\subsection{Mesh-Aware Offline Planning}

Table~\ref{tab:offline-results} summarizes the best feasible method in the
high-coverage mesh experiment. The proposed set-cover CW tour reaches 98.33\%
coverage over 180 mesh targets, exceeding the 70\% success threshold and
nearly reaching the 98\% stop target. The planner selects 23 viewpoints from
357 candidates and produces a 1840~s trajectory with 920 samples. Unlike the
pure coverage-greedy baseline, every selected transfer is feasible and the
minimum clearance remains positive.

\subsection{Method Comparison}

Table~\ref{tab:method-results} reports the deterministic mesh comparison. All
methods except the infeasible coverage-greedy baseline maintain positive
clearance. The proposed set-cover CW tour has the lowest $\Delta v$ among the
methods that simultaneously achieve 98.33\% coverage and remain feasible. It
uses 64.3\% less $\Delta v$ than the random-safe baseline and 40.99\% less
$\Delta v$ than safe coverage-greedy while retaining a much larger minimum
clearance.

\subsection{Trajectory, Attitude, and ROS Validation}

The high-coverage experiment exports both trajectory and camera-attitude
streams. For the set-cover CW tour, \texttt{trajectory.csv} and
\texttt{attitude.csv} each contain 920 samples, while \texttt{viewpoints.csv}
contains the selected camera viewpoints, boresight vectors, yaw, pitch, and
quaternion orientations. These files are used by the trajectory replay node to
publish odometry, path markers, field-of-view markers, and station mesh
markers for RViz and Gazebo validation.

The existing ROS execution smoke run remains a middleware validation artifact.
It records trajectory, control, coverage, planner, safety, summary, manifest,
and rosbag outputs. The run is shorter than the high-coverage offline
trajectory, so its online coverage ratio is not used as the headline planning
result. Its role is to demonstrate that the ROS stack records paper-grade
topics and that Gazebo visualization consumes the ROS state stream while HCW
dynamics remain the source of truth.
